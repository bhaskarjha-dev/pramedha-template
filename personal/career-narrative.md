# Career Narrative — Your Story

> **Purpose:** The prepared answer to "Walk me through your career."
> Maps to: `PramedhaProfile.personal.career_narrative`
>
> ⚠️ PRIVATE — This is your internal script. Adapt for each audience.
>
> Your resume is a list of facts. This is the STORY that connects them.
> Without this, interviewers hear disconnected bullet points.
> With this, they hear a compelling journey with a clear direction.

## The 2-Minute Version (Interview Opening)

> Structure: **Origin → Turning Point → Growth → Current → Future**
>
> - **Where I started:** (education, first role, what drew you in)
> - **The turning point:** (the moment your career direction crystallized)
> - **How I grew:** (key roles, biggest challenges, what you learned)
> - **Where I am now:** (current expertise, what makes you valuable)
> - **Where I'm heading:** (what you're looking for next, why THIS role)

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

- ❌ Reciting your resume chronologically (boring, no insight)
- ❌ Being vague ("I worked on various projects")
- ❌ Negativity about past roles ("My manager was terrible")
- ❌ Too long (over 3 minutes without prompting)
- ✅ Show intentionality — every move was a conscious choice
- ✅ Connect past to present to future
- ✅ Highlight growth, not just titles
